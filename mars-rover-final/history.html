Spirit Rover Mission Timeline

NASA's Spirit rover was one of the twin Mars Exploration Rovers sent to study the geology and history of water on Mars. Spirit far exceeded its planned 90-day mission and operated for more than six years.

June 10, 2003 — Launch

Spirit launched aboard a Delta II rocket from Cape Canaveral, Florida. The rover began its seven-month journey to Mars.

January 4, 2004 — Mars Landing

Spirit successfully landed inside Gusev Crater using airbags and a parachute landing system.

2004 — Discovery of Water Evidence

Spirit discovered minerals that formed in the presence of water, proving Mars once had wet environments.

2005 — Climbing Husband Hill

Spirit climbed Husband Hill, one of the tallest hills explored by a Mars rover at the time.

2009 — Rover Becomes Stuck

Spirit became trapped in soft Martian soil, limiting its mobility but continuing scientific observations.

2010 — Final Communication

NASA lost communication with Spirit after harsh Martian winter conditions affected its solar-powered systems.
