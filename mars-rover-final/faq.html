Frequently Asked Questions

The Spirit rover helped scientists learn about the history of water and geology on Mars. Below are some common questions about the mission.

What was the purpose of the Spirit rover?

Spirit’s mission was to study rocks and soil on Mars and search for evidence that water once existed on the planet.

When did Spirit launch and land on Mars?

Spirit launched on June 10, 2003 and landed on Mars on January 4, 2004.

Where did Spirit land?

Spirit landed inside Gusev Crater, an area scientists believed may have once contained water.

What discoveries did Spirit make?

Spirit found strong evidence that liquid water once existed on Mars. The rover discovered minerals formed in wet conditions and studied volcanic rocks across the Martian surface.

How was Spirit powered?

Spirit used solar panels to generate electricity for its scientific instruments, cameras, and communication systems.

How long did Spirit operate?

Spirit was originally designed for a 90-day mission but continued operating for over six years before communication was lost in 2010.

Why did NASA lose contact with Spirit?

Spirit became stuck in soft soil and later stopped communicating after a harsh Martian winter reduced available solar power.

Why are Mars rovers important?

Mars rovers help scientists study the planet’s climate, geology, and potential for ancient life while preparing for future human exploration.
