About the Mars Rover Program

NASA’s Mars Rover Program explores the surface of Mars using robotic vehicles. These rovers study the planet’s geology, climate, and evidence of ancient water and microbial life.

Spirit Rover

Mission Overview

Spirit launched on June 10, 2003 and landed on Mars on January 4, 2004. The rover was designed to search for geological evidence that water once existed on Mars.

Major Discoveries

  • Discovered signs of ancient hot springs
  • Found evidence of past water activity
  • Captured thousands of Martian images

Technical Features

Solar Panels
Spirit used solar energy to power its systems and instruments.
Panoramic Camera
Allowed scientists to study the Martian landscape in detail.
Rock Abrasion Tool
Used to grind into rocks for scientific analysis.

Curiosity Rover

Mission Overview

Curiosity launched on November 26, 2011 and landed in Gale Crater on August 6, 2012. Its mission is to determine whether Mars ever had conditions capable of supporting microbial life.

Major Discoveries

  1. Detected organic molecules in Martian rocks
  2. Studied ancient lake environments
  3. Measured radiation levels for future human missions

Technical Features

Nuclear Power Source
Curiosity uses a radioisotope thermoelectric generator instead of solar power.
Laser Instrument
Can vaporize rock samples to study chemical composition.
Sky Crane Landing System
Used an advanced landing system to safely place the rover on Mars.
